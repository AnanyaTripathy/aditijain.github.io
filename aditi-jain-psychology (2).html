About Aditi

A space for you to
just be

I'm Aditi, a psychologist with 2 years of clinical experience. I work from a trauma-informed lens, meaning that everything we do together is grounded in emotional safety, consent, and your own pace.

I believe therapy works best when it feels like a genuine relationship — not a set of techniques applied from a distance. My approach draws from relational and person-centered thinking, as well as psychoanalytic ideas that help us understand the deeper patterns shaping our lives.

I offer queer-affirmative and inclusive therapy, and strive to create a space where all identities, experiences, and ways of being are genuinely welcomed and respected.

You bring the curiosity. I bring the care. Together, we find a way through.

Free Tools for You

Simple grounding
techniques

When your nervous system feels overwhelmed, these quick practices can help bring you back to the present moment.

🌿
1–2 minutes

5-4-3-2-1 Senses

Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste. Anchors you in the present moment immediately.

🌬️
3–5 minutes

Box Breathing

Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold for 4. Repeat. This activates your parasympathetic nervous system.

🪨
1 minute

Feel Your Feet

Press your feet firmly into the floor. Notice the texture, temperature, and pressure. Let gravity hold you for a moment.

🌊
2–3 minutes

Cold Water Reset

Splash cool water on your face or hold ice. The temperature shift signals safety to your brain and slows a racing heart.

🌸
5 minutes

Safe Place Visualisation

Close your eyes and imagine a place — real or imagined — where you feel completely safe. Notice every detail with your senses.

Common Questions

You might be
wondering…

  • The first session is a gentle introduction — a chance for us to meet, for you to share what's brought you here, and for me to understand what you're looking for. There's no pressure to dive into anything you're not ready for. We'll also talk about how therapy might work for you and whether I feel like a good fit. It's okay to take your time.

  • Most people find weekly sessions work best, especially at the start — it builds a rhythm and allows us to stay connected to what's unfolding. Sessions are 50 minutes long. As for how long therapy takes, it's deeply personal. Some people find a few months transformative; others prefer longer-term work. We'll check in regularly and you'll always have a say in pacing.

  • Yes, all sessions are currently conducted online via video call. Many people find that online therapy is just as meaningful and connected as in-person work — sometimes more so, because you're in your own space. All you need is a private, comfortable spot and a stable internet connection. I use a secure, confidential platform for all sessions.

  • Absolutely. You don't need to be in crisis to benefit from therapy. People come for all sorts of reasons — feeling stuck, navigating a big life change, wanting to understand themselves better, or simply wanting a space that's just for them. If something feels heavy or confusing, that's enough of a reason. You deserve support regardless of how 'serious' it seems.

  • Yes — everything you share in therapy is held in strict confidence. The only exceptions are situations where there is immediate risk of serious harm to yourself or someone else. I'll explain all of this clearly in our first session so you know exactly what to expect, and you can always ask me questions about confidentiality at any point.

  • Yes, and it's something I care deeply about. I practice queer-affirmative therapy, which means I don't just 'accept' LGBTQIA+ identities — I genuinely celebrate and value them. I'm aware of the specific challenges, histories, and joys that come with queer experience, and I work hard to ensure my space is one where all parts of you are truly welcome.

  • Session fees are listed on my Topmate profile.
