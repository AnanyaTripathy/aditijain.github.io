About Aditi

A space to be
fully yourself

I'm Aditi — a psychologist with 2 years of clinical experience. My work is rooted in the belief that therapy is a deeply collaborative journey, shaped by your unique experiences, needs, and pace.

I work through a trauma-informed lens, meaning I prioritise your emotional safety, respect your boundaries, and approach every conversation with care and attunement. I draw from relational therapy, person-centered practice, and psychoanalytic thinking to help you explore patterns and understand yourself more deeply.

My practice is queer-affirmative and inclusive — a space where LGBTQIA+ identities and experiences are not just welcomed, but truly honoured. Whoever you are, you are welcome here.

How I work

My therapy approach

Trauma-informed care

Safety and consent come first. Every part of our work together is led by what feels right for you — no pressure, no rushing.

Queer-affirmative practice

LGBTQIA+ identities and experiences are affirmed and centred here — not treated as something to explain or overcome.

Relational therapy

We explore the patterns you carry in relationships — with others and with yourself — and how these shape your inner world.

Person-centered approach

You are the expert on your own life. Therapy moves at your pace, guided by what you need — not a set agenda.

Psychoanalytic perspective

We gently explore the deeper layers of your inner world — unconscious patterns and feelings that quietly shape your experiences.

Common questions

Frequently asked questions

It's completely normal to have questions before starting therapy. Here are some of the things people often wonder about.

Getting started

Beginning therapy can feel daunting, and that's completely okay. You don't need to arrive with a clear agenda or the "right words." The first session is simply a space to meet each other — to talk about what brought you here, what you're hoping for, and to get a feel for how we might work together. There's no pressure to have everything figured out before you begin.

Getting started

You don't need to be in crisis to benefit from therapy. Many people come when they're feeling stuck, overwhelmed, curious about themselves, or simply want a dedicated space to think and reflect. If you've been considering it, that curiosity itself is often a good enough reason to begin.

Sessions

The first session is an opportunity for us to get to know each other. I might ask about what's been on your mind, a little about your background and history, and what you're hoping to get from therapy. It's also a chance for you to ask me anything. We'll use this session to decide together whether working together feels like the right fit.

Sessions

Sessions are typically 50 minutes long. Most people find it helpful to meet weekly, especially at the start — consistency creates a rhythm that supports the work. That said, frequency is always something we can discuss and adapt to what feels right for you.

Sessions

There's no fixed timeline. Some people find what they need in a few months; others choose to continue for longer as the work deepens. The duration depends on what you're bringing, what feels meaningful to you, and how the process unfolds. We'll check in regularly about how things are going.

Approach

Trauma-informed practice means I approach everything with an awareness of how painful experiences shape a person. In practice, it means:

  • We never go faster than feels safe for you
  • You always have the right to pause, redirect, or not go somewhere
  • I won't push you to talk about things before you're ready
  • Your responses — including avoidance or silence — are understood, not judged
